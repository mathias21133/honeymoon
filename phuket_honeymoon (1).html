Base — The Shore at Katathani

The Shore at Katathani · Kata Noi Beach · Phuket

Why This Resort

The Shore is the adults-only boutique wing of the Katathani resort, sitting at the quieter southern end of Kata Noi beach — one of the most beautiful bays in Phuket and far less crowded than Patong or Kata Yai. The villas have private infinity pools facing the Andaman. This is exactly the right base for a honeymoon built around recovery and luxury — you're not here to sightsee, you're here to be still. Three full resort days bookend two excursions. The pacing is deliberate.

Dining Hours — Confirmed

Orora Restaurant (The Shore Beach Club, beachside) serves dinner 6pm–11pm, last order 10:30pm. Param Para (the resort's signature romantic Thai restaurant) serves dinner 6:30pm–midnight, last order 10:30pm. Both are open when you arrive on Day 1 at 7:30pm — no rush, no scramble. The Harbor restaurant serves breakfast and all-day dining. Room service runs 24 hours.

Spa — Serenity Spa at The Shore

Book both spa sessions at check-in before they fill up. Session 1 on Day 2 (traditional Thai massage — right after the long-haul flight). Session 2 on Day 5 (couples' treatment — the luxury counterpoint to sleeping in a jungle bungalow the night before). Mention it's your honeymoon at booking; the team often adds gestures. Two sessions minimum — a third on Day 3 morning is easy to slot if you want it.

Getting Around

Grab works everywhere in Phuket. The resort can also arrange private drivers for the night market (Day 3, ~20–30 min), Old Town (Day 5, ~30 min), and the Khao Sok transfer (operator-arranged as part of the package). For everything else you don't need a car — you're at the beach.

🏮6:00pm
Night Market · Dinner on Foot
Night Market Dinner — Walk the Whole Thing First ★★
The move at any Thai night market: don't order at the first stall you see. Walk the entire market once, identify the four or five things you want, then circle back. What to eat: pad thai from a wok cart (fresh, never from a pan sitting on low heat — watch for the flames), moo ping (grilled pork skewers with sticky rice), grilled corn in coconut milk, kanom jeen (rice noodles in fish curry broth — Phuket's local specialty), mango sticky rice last. Wash it down with fresh sugarcane juice or a Singha from the corner vendor. Bring ฿500–800 cash between you — you won't spend all of it but you'll want options.
Walk full market first · Cash only · ฿500–800 between two for a full dinner · Mango sticky rice is always the last thing, not the first
Cha yen (Thai iced tea — orange, sweet, with condensed milk in a bag with a straw) is mandatory. One each, at any cart. It costs ฿25 and tastes exactly like Thailand.

Arrival
Boat Transfer to Floating Bungalow ★★ The Arrival
The boat ride across Cheow Lan Lake is one of the best arrivals of the trip. The lake is emerald-green — man-made, flooded for a dam in 1982 — and surrounded by 400-million-year-old karst limestone towers rising straight from the water, taller and more dramatic than Halong Bay. Dinner on the Lake + Night Under Stars ★★

☕5:00pm
Coffee · Old Town Specialty
Bookhemian or Campus Coffee Roaster ★ Start Here
Two excellent options within a short walk of each other on Thalang Road. Bookhemian (45/1 Thalang Rd) occupies a beautifully decayed Sino-Portuguese shophouse — wooden tables, art-covered walls, photography books, a hidden upstairs gallery that often has local exhibitions. Order the iced latte with coconut milk and a slice of banana cake. Time slows down here, which is the point. Campus Coffee Roaster is one of Phuket's first specialty roasters, in a Sino-Portuguese building, sourcing single-origin beans from northern Thailand (Doi Saket, Doi Chang) — ask for the pour-over. Either one is the right opening move. Graph Coffee, also on Thalang Rd, is a third option with excellent canelé pastries alongside serious espresso.
Bookhemian: 45/1 Thalang Rd · Campus Coffee Roaster: Thalang Rd area · Graph Coffee: Thalang Rd · All walk-in · Opens from 8:30am (still serving at 5pm)
🏛5:45pm
Architecture · The Walk
Thalang Rd → Soi Romanee → Dibuk Rd → Phang Nga Rd ★★
The Phuket Old Town is one of the best-preserved Sino-Portuguese towns in Southeast Asia — 19th-century shophouses built by Hokkien Chinese and Straits Malay merchants: five-foot covered walkways, tiled facades, shuttered windows, interior courtyards. Walk it without a map. Thalang Road is the spine — dense with cafes, shrines, and old storefronts. Soi Romanee is the most atmospheric lane: narrow, pastel-painted, lined with murals depicting old Phuket life (look for the large-format Joe Boys street art on building walls). Dibuk Road has the grandest old merchant houses, including the Blue Elephant mansion — the former governor's residence, now a restaurant, but you can walk the grounds and browse the souvenir shop freely. Phang Nga Road has the Thai Hua Museum (30 min, tells the story of how Phuket became Phuket — housed in a gorgeous old Chinese-school building). Oasis Walking Street is a narrow covered passage connecting Thalang and Phang Nga — full of local craft stalls and snack vendors, a good escape from afternoon heat or rain. Walk slowly. Turn down alleys that look interesting. Look up at the signage and facade tiles.
Thalang Rd → Soi Romanee (off Thalang) → Dibuk Rd → Phang Nga Rd · On foot, 30–40 min end to end at a wandering pace · Thai Hua Museum: Krabi Rd, ~฿200 entry · Blue Elephant grounds: free to enter
China Inn on Thalang Rd is worth stepping into: simultaneously a restaurant with excellent pork dishes, a café, and a shop selling vintage furniture and Peranakan antiques. You don't have to eat there — the space itself is worth seeing. The interior courtyard is one of the most beautiful rooms in Old Town.
🍽7:00pm
Dinner · Old Town
Suay Restaurant or Raya Restaurant or Kopitiam ★★
Three options at different registers. Suay (contemporary Thai, Dibuk Rd, book ahead) is the best sit-down dinner in the Old Town — refined, locally-sourced, the most accomplished cooking in the neighborhood. Raya Restaurant (century-old mosaic-tiled floors, Michelin-recognized) is the old-school institution — their yellow crab curry is the signature, order it. Kopitiam (Thalang Rd, walk-in, affordable) is the most local and casual — excellent everyday Thai-Chinese dishes that workers in the neighborhood have been eating for generations. Any of these is the right dinner for the evening. If the queue at Raya is too long, Kopitiam next door will not disappoint.
Suay: 50/2 Takua Pa Rd · Book 1 week ahead · ~฿600–900/person · Raya: Dibuk Rd · Walk-in or call ahead · Michelin · Yellow crab curry · Kopitiam: Thalang Rd · Walk-in · ~฿150–250/person
If you want street food instead of a sit-down: the vendors on Thalang Rd and the surrounding lanes do kanom jeen (Phuket's signature rice noodle in fish curry broth), moo hong (slow-braised pork belly, intensely savory), and fried oyster omelette. Point at whatever is coming off the wok. Cash only, ~฿80–120 per dish.
🛍Post-dinner
Shopping · After Dinner
Old Town Shops — While It's Still Light Enough
The Old Town shops stay open until 7–9pm. After dinner, browse what's still open. The best boutiques for local goods: Southwind Books (secondhand books, maps, Phuket history texts), the craft stalls in Oasis Walking Street (handmade jewelry, batik fabric, Peranakan ceramics), and the antique shops on Phang Nga Road (old tin-mining memorabilia, vintage Straits-era porcelain, colonial maps). Loveramics and local ceramics shops are worth checking for coffee cups and small objects to carry into the rest of the trip. The shopping here is genuinely local — not the souvenir-market version you get in Patong.
Oasis Walking Street: between Thalang and Phang Nga Rd · Southwind Books: Thalang Rd area · Most shops open until 8–9pm · Cash or card depending on vendor

🏛 Old Town Architecture + Sights

Soi Romanee ★★The most photographed lane in Phuket — narrow, pastel shophouses, murals by Joe Boys collective depicting old Phuket life. Off Thalang Rd. Always open, free.
Thai Hua Museum ★ · Krabi RdTells the story of Phuket's tin-mining era and Hokkien Chinese heritage. Housed in a 1930s Chinese school building. ~฿200 entry. 28 Krabi Rd. Allow 30–45 min.
Blue Elephant Mansion · Phang Nga RdThe former governor's residence — magnificent Colonial-Sino mansion in a private park. Now a restaurant and cooking school. Walk the grounds and souvenir shop freely.
Oasis Walking StreetNarrow covered passage connecting Thalang and Phang Nga Rd. Local craft stalls, snacks, shade from heat and rain. Good browse between streets.
Dibuk RoadThe grandest merchant houses and mansion facades in Old Town. Walk the full length slowly and look up at the tile work and shuttered windows.
Wat Mongkol Nimit · Soi Romanee endActive Buddhist temple at the end of Soi Romanee. Locals come regularly — ceremonies, offerings, monks. Enter quietly and respectfully. Free.

🛍 Old Town Shopping

Oasis Walking Street stallsHandmade jewelry, batik fabric, Peranakan ceramics, local craft objects. Between Thalang and Phang Nga Rd. Best browsing in Old Town.
China Inn shop · Thalang RdVintage furniture, Peranakan antiques, Straits-era objects. Inside the China Inn compound — even if you skip the restaurant, browse the shop.
Southwind Books · Thalang Rd areaSecondhand books, old maps, Phuket history texts, Southeast Asia travel writing. Good for a paperback to carry into the rest of the trip.
Antique shops · Phang Nga RdTin-mining memorabilia, vintage Straits-era porcelain, colonial maps and prints. Phang Nga Rd has the best concentration.
Ceramics boutiques · MultipleLocally-made Peranakan-style ceramics (hand-painted, bold colors) are unique to the Straits region. Good small objects to carry forward in the trip.

🏮 Night Markets

Chillva Market ★ · Phuket TownClosest to Old Town and the resort. ~80 vendors, string lights, local crowd, contained and manageable. Yaowarat Rd. Opens ~5pm, best 6–9pm. ~฿500–800/couple for a full dinner. ~20 min by Grab from Kata Noi.
Bang Niang Night Market ★★ · Khok KloiLarger (200+ stalls), ~35 min north. More comprehensive food selection, more local feel. Mon/Thu traditional nights, Saturday is biggest. ~฿500–800/couple. Worth the extra drive for the scale.

🌿 Khao Sok Operators

Elephant Hills ★★ · PremiumLuxury tented camp and floating villa on Cheow Lan Lake. Best operator. All-inclusive, private accommodation, higher service level. elephanthills.com. ~$500–600/person for 2D/1N.
Our Jungle House ★ · Mid-rangeWell-run floating bungalow camp. Includes pickup from Phuket, kayaking, all meals. ourjunglehouse.com. ~$200–350/person.
Khao Sok National Park Tours · Budget/MidMultiple verified operators at khaosok.com. Confirm package covers Cheow Lan Lake overnight specifically — not just the river section of the park.

📋 Practical Notes

CurrencyThai Baht (฿). Withdraw ฿6,000–8,000 cash on arrival — night markets and street stalls are cash only. ATMs at the airport; Kasikorn Bank and Bangkok Bank ATMs have lowest fees. Always pay in Thai Baht at ATMs, never home currency (dynamic currency conversion is a scam).
TransportGrab is the standard ride-hailing app and works well across Phuket. Always agree on a price before getting in unmetered taxis. Resort drivers for Khao Sok transfer included in your operator package — confirm pickup time 48 hrs before.
TDAC — Thailand Digital Arrival CardRequired for all non-Thai nationals entering Thailand by air. Fill out before flying from New York at thaiPass.go.th. One per traveler. Free. Do this before you leave home.
eSIM / DataBuy an eSIM before departure (Airalo — Thailand plan ~$5–8) or pick up a True Move H or AIS tourist SIM at HKT airport arrivals (~฿300–400 for 30-day unlimited data). Note: there is no signal at Cheow Lan Lake — embrace it.
WeatherLate May: 30–34°C, humid, afternoon rain likely. Mornings are clear — beach and activity time before noon. Rain is brief and heavy, not a reason to cancel plans. Pack light rain gear or buy a poncho at 7-Eleven for ฿60.
HKT AirportPhuket International Airport, ~45 min north of Kata Noi. Allow 2.5 hours before international departure. Arrange private transfer through the resort the evening before your flight.
TippingRestaurants: ฿20–50/person. Spa therapists: ฿100–200 each. Taxi: round up to nearest ฿20. Night market stalls: not expected.
